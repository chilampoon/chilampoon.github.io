Tools for rhythmicity detection

Author: Chi Lam Poon Date: December 31, 2019  12:33:35

Introduction

In early years, some tools can only detect rhythmicity, i.e. to tell you whether this gene or transcript or protein had a cycling expression, and they cannot tell you whether the feature you tested had different amplitudes, phases, etc. in different conditions. Thus, some tools were later created for doing that and they called this process differential rhythmicity.

There are two main types of methods, parametric and nonparametric. The difference between these two is just whether based on a known mathematical model or not.

Parametric methods are able to detect weaker rhythms than nonparametric methods. However, parametric methods rely on stricter assumptions: usually, measurement errors must have Gaussian distributions, and the methods are sensible to outliers.

Nonparametric methods sacrifice some degree of power in order to gain robustness against outliers, and they make fewer assumptions about measurement errors.
