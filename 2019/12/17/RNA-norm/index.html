    Normalization methods for RNA-seq

    Chi Lam Poon
    2019-12-17
    RNA-seq

    Why normalization?

    I usually make the reasons for every implementation or analysis clear and this is much more important than knowing how to do something.

    The reason to do normalization is very clear: to FAIRLY compare the expression values within or between samples. In this article, the author explained quite well, however I found something wrong in the table of normalization methods, so be careful! Mainly, these are factors need to be considered (images were borrowed from that post):

    Within sample:

    • Gene length: longer gene would have more counts

    • GC-content: GC-rich and GC-poor fragments tend to be under-represented

    • Sequence position: RNA degradation, I am still not sure about it, explain it later

    Between sample:

    • Sequencing depth: the total amout of RNA varies in each sample

    • RNA composition: maybe some sample-specific genes highly expressed or contamination
