A lunch conversation

Author: Chi Lam Poon Date: August 22, 2019  20:59:24

Today Silvia treated me a lunch, since I am gonna leave US soon.

As a start, I mention about my trip to New York City and Philadelphia. I say I saw so many religious sculptures and pictures in Metropolitan Museum at NYC, while I don’t quite understand meanings behind them. Then Silvia starts to tell me the stories about Jesus and beliefs of them because she is a Christian.

I’ll briefly describe what she says. We talk in Chinese so I don’t know if I use the words or jargons correctly. The world we live was created by God, and the Blessed Trinity - the Father, the Son (Jesus Christ), and the Holy Spirit - are alternities of this one God. Jesus was born to save everyone (Christian only) from the penalty of sin by his death, as everyone will have many sins all over lifetime. What is sin? In her view, she belives that not only the bad actions that break laws but also the impulse to hit/kill someone or the time that one cannot control him/herself to do something which should not be done or something like that. Therefore, if you choose to become an Christian, you will go the heaven after death because you can recover the relationship between you and the God (it means save the sins), otherwise you just go to hell…

I am a little bit suprise they Christians firmly believe that human beings are created by the God (not by evolution) but she agrees with the micro evolution. That’s so strange because I think what moves forward micro evolution also promotes macro evolution. Every creature starts from the very first-appeared DNAs, and that “selfish” DNA assembled more protein complexs and stucures forming a cell then more and more complicated organs and finally an organism, all these are to duplicate themselves so that they (and their offsprings) can continue to exist in this world. And “selfish” is exactly the reason of evolution, no matter micro or macro.

What’s more important, there are so many ideas from Christians are same as those I acquire/think from my life experience. For example, everything happening in this world is pre-arranged already, many events are not controlled by yourself but something else. Thus I think this “something else” in my mind is equal to the God that Christians believe. Also, Silvia says the arragements for everyone composes the best route of life because of the love from the God. That’s what I trust as well - every creature will find their own way - no matter how many time lines or parallel worlds, the one I am in is the best. Well, they can treat the essential events, such as PhD application or something deadly important peacefully, this is the thing I need to learn, maybe I am still too young.

Finally I ask Silvia the opinion of Christian about human’s consciousness. She replys me by repeating the concepts stated before: the God loves people. Therefore he gives people their own “free will”, rather than making people like something running under codes, and following some rules. And freedom is not letting you do whatever you wanna but not to do the things you don’t wanna do. She argues that the freedom is from saving the sins with the help of God, and it (saving sins, getting free?) is also the main purpose of a person living in the world. Hmmm.. at the moment I think I just want to find out what on earth your GOD is and why the world is as it is, that’s the purpose for me to live on the earth.

This is the first time I have a general overview of the religion, before that I know religions are about the wise summary of life experience and some answers of questions about human beings and this universe, oh I will definitely read more religious books.
