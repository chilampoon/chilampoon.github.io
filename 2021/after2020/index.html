The First Post-2020 Post

Author: Chi Lam Poon Date: April 11, 2021  17:40:47

2020 was the most brutal year in my life because the then future was extremely unforeseeable and unstable. Later when I was waiting for my goddamn visa, I heard that my ex-advisor got colon cancer and was suffering in the hospital. I was so shocked and sad for a few days. He is a very nice person and was the closest professor in my early college life, although we didn’t keep in touch afterward… Anyways, life and death are pre-ordained. What we can do is to support others.

And I found that if I am continually nervous or anxious, I am not able to focus. So, I played Zelda all the time ( ͡° ͜ʖ ͡°) Once the source of anxiety is gone, the brain still needs time (~1 or 2 months?) to recover, which means my mind still easily gets distracted. Some other people on the internet also said that they could not work or study well when feeling anxious. I guess the brain’s reaction to this kind of negative emotion is releasing some weird substances, probably neurotransmitters or something, to keep the body ‘unbusy’. When the pressure source is removed, the body will gradually degrade these substances, so there’s a recovery period, interesting bs…

Hard to believe that I’ve been through all the shits and am living ‘stably’ in 2021… There was also something happy in 2020 - I participated in VCL and GSoC in the summer. It was such a relief that being busy and communicating with my teammates and mentors, and learning exciting stuff. Also, I just received my first dose of COVID vaccine yesterday (could only choose Pfizer :/). My arm got sore 3 hours later, other than that, I don’t feel any uncomfortable or tired. It’s either ineffective or lucky, but have to wait until the second dose to see the effects.

Miss the sea a lot, hope that I can go scuba diving again and finish the kite surfing course soon😢

For what? That I stay for two hundred years looking at cruelty and corruption,
that I see each day through to its end. Each night through till dawn,
that each dawn from behind the window
I see the mocking face of the sun and look at another day
with immense disgust before bitter tea has touched my lips,
then once again the writhing squirming struggle.
That I go over the tale once again of the book of Balkh’s poet,
a cage, the whole world a cage, a cage.
I think of fleeing, of pulling my cloak round my body,
my head scarf over my hair to the streets of nowhere.
In the midst of depravity and misery, in this smoke,
this sorrow for all that is and is not.
I begin my complaint against oppression.
Although you’ve called me again all our friends are suffering.
Shall I leave them in the midst of disaster?
For what? That I enjoy myself again.
For what? That your good doctors make me well again and I take ths risk,
suitcase in hand that I’m ready to travel again, that I come, and my heart is renewed.
That I come with my eyes unclouded.
That I come and among your people I once again make a stir with my poems.
But I haven’t fallen into this snowy cloud in such a way that I’ll get out again.
I don’t imagine I’ll reach safety, that I’ll emerge from this profound disaster.
My old friend, dear friend, leave me in this dream of winter.
It’s possible, who knows, that I can soothe my soul and body.
If a gently spring breeze bringing the green of new growth should waft across my dried-up nerves,
my body might bear fruit.

For What?
Simin Behbahani
