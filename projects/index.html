Independent research projects:

Circadian rhythms of transcriptionally regulatory events across mammalian tissues

I recently proposed an interesting idea for isoform analyses of circadian patterns. I aim to use conventional bulk RNA-seq to identify events of alternative promoter, alternative splicing, and alternative polyadenylation, then characterize their circadian rhythms. Besides, I want to explore their relationships, in other words, how these events integrate each other to eventually present an overall circadian rhythm together with the gene expression level. Hope get some exciting findings!

Collaborated projects:

Characterize the 3’ UTR landscape in human heck and neck cancer cell line under ER-stress

This is the collaboration with Dr. Kaifeng Hung’s lab. They have some interesting results on ER-stressed heck and neck cancer cells and want to see the 3’ UTRome changes in these cells. In addition, a study proved that PD-L1 expression is changed due to its 3’ UTR disruption, thus we also want to specificially look into genes associated with immume therapy.
Here is my writing sample of the pipeline I built when waiting for the RNA-seq data: APA_workflow_Chilam.

A review of tools identifying polyA sites from RNA-seq

Beginning with the HNSC project, me and other lab members implemented different packages for identifying and predicting alternative polyadenylation sites using RNA-seq, such as DaPars, ChangePoint, QAPA, TAPAS, etc. We are planning to write a review for them.
